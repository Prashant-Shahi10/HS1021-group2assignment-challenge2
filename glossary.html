Glossary

Welcome to our photography glossary. Here you'll find definitions for common photography terms to help you better understand the technical aspects of photography.

Key Terms

  • Aperture: The opening in a camera lens through which light enters. It affects depth of field and exposure.
  • ISO: Measures the sensitivity of the camera sensor to light. Higher ISO settings allow for shooting in low light but can introduce noise.
  • Shutter Speed: The length of time the camera's shutter is open, allowing light to hit the sensor. It affects motion blur and exposure.
  • Exposure: The amount of light that reaches the camera sensor, determined by aperture, shutter speed, and ISO.
  • White Balance: Adjusts the colors to match the lighting conditions, ensuring that whites appear white and other colors are accurate.

Advanced Concepts

  • Bokeh: The pleasing out-of-focus areas in a photo, often seen as soft, rounded highlights. Used to create a sense of depth.
  • Depth of Field: The range of distance within a photo that appears sharp. Influenced by aperture, focal length, and distance from the subject.
  • HDR (High Dynamic Range): Combines multiple exposures to capture a wide range of light and detail.
  • RAW Files: Unprocessed image files that retain all the data captured by the camera sensor, offering more flexibility in post-processing.
  • Rule of Thirds: A composition principle that divides the frame into a 3x3 grid. Placing key elements along these lines or their intersections creates a balanced image.
